# References Directory

## Purpose
This directory stores reference materials for your video script projects. You can organize:
- Research and competitor analysis
- Personal style guides and writing templates
- Custom workflow templates
- Temporary files and draft ideas
- Any materials to inspire and support your video creation

## Recommended Structure

```
references/
├── videos/                         # Reference videos
│   ├── video-analysis-1.md
│   └── competitor-breakdown.md
├── accounts/                       # Competitor accounts
│   ├── channel-analysis-1.md
│   └── top-creators.md
├── research/                       # General research
│   ├── market-report.md
│   └── industry-trends.md
├── templates/                      # Personal templates (optional)
│   ├── my-hook-template.md
│   ├── my-opening-style.md
│   └── custom-structure.md
├── styles/                         # Writing style guides (optional)
│   ├── brand-voice.md
│   └── tone-guidelines.md
└── temp/                           # Temporary files (optional)
    ├── draft-ideas.md
    └── quick-notes.md
```

## Usage Tips

### Core Directories (Auto-created)
1. **Videos**: Store analysis of reference videos - what works, what doesn't, and why
2. **Accounts**: Track competitor channels - their content strategy, audience, and performance
3. **Research**: General research materials, market reports, industry insights

### Optional Directories (Create as needed)
4. **Templates**: Your personal script templates, hook formulas, opening patterns
   - Save your proven frameworks for reuse
   - Customize structures that work for your niche
5. **Styles**: Personal writing style guides
   - Define your brand voice and tone
   - Set guidelines for consistency across videos
6. **Temp**: Temporary files, quick notes, draft ideas
   - Brain dumps and unstructured thoughts
   - Files you're not sure where to organize yet

### How to Add References
- Manually add files to appropriate subdirectories
- Create additional subdirectories based on your needs
- Reference materials can be accessed during Stage 3 (Content Research)
- Use Markdown format for easy reading and editing
- The AI agent can read files from this directory when you mention them
